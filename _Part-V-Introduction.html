    Part V — The Danish Context

    Why Danish Context Matters

    This course is designed for a Danish gymnasium and has been shaped by conversations with Danish educators. It would be a mistake to treat the competency frameworks and pedagogical strategies as universal, context-neutral ideas. They are not. They are situated in a specific educational philosophy—the Danish and Nordic tradition of dannelse—and a specific policy landscape shaped by recent reforms.

    Understanding this context is important for three reasons. First, it explains why competency development is not merely a compliance exercise with the IB curriculum, but an authentic expression of what Danish education aims to do. Second, it provides philosophical grounding: when teachers understand that they are developing competencies in service of human formation, the work becomes more meaningful. Third, it offers practical guidance: the Danish educational reforms and examples like Silkeborg Gymnasium show how competency development can actually be implemented in a way that is coherent with school culture and values.

    What Part V Contains

    Chapter 29 — Dannelse and Character Formation opens with the foundational concept of dannelse—the holistic formation of persons across intellectual, moral, social, civic, and cultural dimensions. This concept, rooted in the European philosophical tradition of Humboldt and Grundtvig, is neither quaint nor outdated. It is the philosophical heart of Danish education law and curriculum. The chapter explains what dannelse means, how it appears in Danish policy, and how it relates to contemporary competency frameworks.

    Chapter 30 — Danish Education Reforms examines two major policy developments that enable and shape competency education in Denmark. The 2014 Folkeskole reform restructured primary and lower-secondary education around competency goals, well-being, and professional collaboration. The emerging epx (practice-oriented gymnasium) reform proposes bridging the historical divide between academic and vocational pathways, with competencies as the bridge. Both reforms are rooted in dannelse philosophy and provide institutional support for the work described in this course.

    Chapter 31 — Case Study: Silkeborg Gymnasium shows what competency development looks like in practice. Silkeborg, Denmark's largest gymnasium, demonstrates how a school can maintain coherent educational vision across many teachers and students, how professional learning communities can sustain pedagogical innovation, and how dannelse extends into new domains like digital education and climate formation. The case study is not presented as a perfect model, but as a concrete example that raises important questions about implementation at scale.

    How to Use Part V

    If you are a Danish educator, Part V should feel familiar in its assumptions and language, while also offering new language and frameworks for thinking about what you are already doing. Use it to connect your existing practice to research and to the broader competency agenda.

    If you are a non-Danish educator teaching in Denmark, Part V provides essential context for understanding your school's values and policy landscape. It shows why certain emphases (well-being alongside achievement, character formation, professional collaboration) are not optional but central to Danish educational philosophy.

    If you are designing a curriculum or professional development programme, Part V shows how to ground competency work in the values and traditions of Danish schools, rather than importing frameworks as external requirements.

    Key Concepts to Remember

    • Dannelse: Formation of the whole person across intellectual, moral, social, civic, and cultural dimensions
    • Trivsel: Well-being as both social belonging and individual flourishing
    • Personlig myndighed: Personal authority or agency—the capacity to direct one's own life responsibly
    • Fællesskab: Community—the sense of belonging and shared purpose
    • Kompetencemål: Competency goals—the framework for what students should be able to do
    • Digital dannelse: Formation in relation to digital technology and AI

    Connections Across the Course

    Part V is grounded in the theory and research of Parts I-IV, which developed the competency frameworks, cognitive science foundations, and pedagogical approaches. Part V shows how these frameworks are instantiated in Danish context. Parts VI and Appendix then move into implementation: how to actually develop these competencies in daily practice.
