    3 Habits to Practice Curiosity — and Escape Your Phone

    Speaker: Nayeema Raza | Event: TEDNext 2025 (November 11, 2025) | Duration: 5:13

    Summary

    Journalist Nayeema Raza argues that our entanglement with devices has created a paradox: we feel more connected when inside our phones than when physically together. She proposes three "old habits" to reclaim curiosity and presence in an AI-driven world:

    1. Pause — Take one second before reaching for your device. Research suggests that waiting even briefly before acting lets your brain work more effectively.
    2. Wonder — Resist the urge to Google every question immediately. Float in your own curiosity instead of drowning in information.
    3. Ask a question out loud — Have real conversations. Ask something of someone you thought you couldn't learn from, or someone you think you know everything about.

    Raza cites early MIT research suggesting that constant phone access is making us "lazier and less smart" and less connected. She frames the talk through an 11-year-old named Sophie who observes that FaceTime feels more connected than being physically together because "nowadays, when you're with them, everyone's on their phones."

    The talk closes with a personal story about Raza's father's passing, where she realised the "old habits" — pausing, wondering, asking small human questions — were what mattered most when devices could not help.
